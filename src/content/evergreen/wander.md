Once upon a time people had ~ blogrolls ~ Well here in 2026, we have wander pages (a la [James’ Coffee Blog](https://jamesg.blog/wander)).

- [A Collection of Unmitigated Pedantry](https://acoup.blog/) by Bret Devereaux: Do you like reading about the Roman Republic? Of course you do. Dr Devereaux is (imo) the leading public intellectual on the classic Mediterranean, not to mention fantasy worldbuilding; his _very_ long posts are scholarly but eminently readable.
- [Alan Jacobs](https://social.ayjay.org/): One of the greatest literary and cultural critics of our time, even if he _occasionally_ presumes the truth of his Christian faith.
- [anhvn](https://anhvn.com/): Anh is a designer (?) based in Vancouver (??) I think (???). But in any case she redesigns her site about once a month and it’s always downright delightful.
- [Futility Closet](https://www.futilitycloset.com/): A charming collection of fun facts and curiosities. Their podcast is dearly missed.
- [Jacob Geller](https://www.youtube.com/@JacobGeller): The most important media critic of our generation, and it’s not even close. You can skip the year’s-best and “games that do x” video essays if you really don’t care about video games, but everything else is essential.
- [Takes™ by Jamelle Bouie](https://www.youtube.com/@jamelle-bouie): The only American political commentator that makes a lick of sense.
- [Quinns Quest](https://www.youtube.com/@Quinns_Quest): I’ve been following “Iron Quinns” since the _Rock, Paper, Shotgun_ days, but he’s truly found his calling nerding out about tabletop role-playing games while engaging in droll English humour. His enthusiasm is so infectious that I a.) watch every episode despite knowing I’ll never play most if any of these games b.) want to go be enthusiastic about all the things I love too.
- [Robin Sloan](https://www.robinsloan.com/): C’mon if you’ve spent ten minutes on this site you know I read every word the man writes.
